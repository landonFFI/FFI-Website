For High-Volume Locations: Outdoor Drive-Up ATMs

Some gas stations run 24 hours. They're on high-traffic corridors. Their customers don't want to park and walk inside to pull cash — they want to pull up, grab cash, and go. For locations like these, FFI offers outdoor drive-up ATM installation — a completely different category of service that almost no ATM company in Alabama actually provides end-to-end.

Here is exactly what FFI handles for an outdoor installation. Every step. Nothing left for you to figure out.

Outdoor Placement Is for Qualifying Locations

During your free site visit, we'll assess whether your location is a good fit. There is no cost to you — for the site visit or the installation.

What You Do

You say yes. You provide the site. You collect a check every month.

That is your entire role in an outdoor installation.

  1. 01
    Site Evaluation

    We assess your location. Traffic count, sight lines, electrical access, ADA compliance, distance requirements from fuel dispensers. We tell you if your location qualifies. You don't guess.

  2. 02
    Machine Selection

    Outdoor installations use machines built specifically for outdoor use — weatherproof, temperature-rated, built to run in Alabama summers and cold snaps. We spec the right equipment for your site.

  3. 03
    Permitting

    Outdoor installations require city permits. Building permits. Electrical permits. Sometimes zoning sign-off. We file every one of them — in Birmingham, Montgomery, and Tuscaloosa. You sign nothing at City Hall.

  4. 04
    Licensed Contractors

    We hire them. We coordinate them. Electricians. Concrete contractors. Any trade required for your specific installation. You do not manage a single subcontractor.

  5. 05
    Electrical Work

    A dedicated weatherproof circuit. Conduit run if needed. Utility coordination if required. Done by licensed professionals FFI coordinates directly.

  6. 06
    Concrete Pad and Bollards

    Your outdoor ATM sits on a poured concrete pad surrounded by protective steel bollards — embedded deep, filled with concrete — to protect the machine and your customers from vehicle impact.

  7. 07
    Machine Installation

    The ATM is anchored, sealed, and secured. If it's a through-the-wall unit, the opening is professionally finished and weatherproofed.

  8. 08
    Signage and Compliance

    Surcharge disclosure. Regulatory compliance. Lighting if needed. Done.

  9. 09
    Inspection

    We schedule and pass the city inspection. ADA review. Final sign-off. FFI handles it.

  10. 10
    Cash Loading and Go-Live

    We load the vault via our cash loading services. We test every transaction. We go live. Your machine is open for business.
