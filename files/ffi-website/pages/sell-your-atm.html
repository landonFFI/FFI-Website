Valuation

What We Look At

Every ATM and every route is different. Here are the factors that affect what we offer. The more of these you have working in your favor, the stronger your position — but we evaluate everything and make offers at every stage.

01
Contracts with your host locations

An active, assignable contract with a clear term and auto-renewal is worth more than a verbal handshake. Long-term contracts with stable merchants signal predictable income to a buyer.

02
Machine type and age

Current EMV-compliant machines in good working condition hold more value. Older non-EMV units are still buyable — they're just priced accordingly. We consider make, model, and condition.

03
Transaction volume history

Consistent monthly transaction data tells us what the machine actually earns. A machine with documented volume history is more valuable than one we're guessing at.

04
Route size and geographic concentration

A cluster of machines within a small geographic area is easier to operate and more valuable to a buyer than the same number spread across a wide region. Concentration matters.

05
Your cash loading setup

An established, documented cash loading arrangement — whether merchant-loaded or through a third party — adds real value. Buyers price in operational simplicity.
