Every ATM company sounds the same on the phone. They'll tell you they handle everything. They'll promise fast response. Then you call on a Friday night and get a ticket number.

For a single-location owner, that's frustrating. For an operator running multiple sites, it's expensive — across every location, compounding every week.

Here is what a poorly-served ATM portfolio costs an operator:

Downtime multiplies

When one machine goes down, you lose surcharge revenue at that location. But you also lose the in-store purchase that customer would have made after pulling cash. Multiply that across five locations and the number gets significant fast.

No one knows your portfolio

Peak nights are when vendors fail you most

A football Saturday in Tuscaloosa. A Friday night on the strip. A holiday weekend at your busiest c-store. National response times average two to four days. That machine doesn't wait.
